<p><p>The cancer drug therapy market encompasses three primary types: targeted therapy, chemotherapy, and immunotherapy. Targeted therapy focuses on specific cancer cell mutations and pathways, minimizing damage to healthy cells. Chemotherapy uses powerful drugs to kill rapidly dividing cells but can impact normal cells, leading to side effects. Immunotherapy enhances the body's immune response against cancer, utilizing various strategies to help detect and destroy cancer cells. These therapies are often combined to improve treatment efficacy and patient outcomes.</p></p>
